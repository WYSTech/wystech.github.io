Zion National Park

Discover the Majesty of Southern Utah

Introduction

Zion National Park, located in southwestern Utah, is renowned for its stunning red rock landscapes, towering cliffs, and deep canyons. Established in 1919, the park encompasses over 230 square miles of protected wilderness, offering visitors a wealth of outdoor recreation opportunities and breathtaking scenery.

The park's unique geology, shaped by millions of years of erosion and uplift, has resulted in a landscape characterized by narrow slot canyons, colorful sandstone formations, and lush riparian areas. From challenging hikes to scenic drives, Zion offers something for every outdoor enthusiast.

Whether you're marveling at the towering walls of Zion Canyon or hiking to the summit of Angels Landing, Zion National Park is sure to leave you awe-struck with its natural beauty and unparalleled vistas.

Major Attractions

Zion Canyon

Zion Canyon is the centerpiece of Zion National Park, with its towering sandstone cliffs rising thousands of feet above the Virgin River. The canyon stretches for 15 miles, offering breathtaking views, scenic hikes, and unparalleled photo opportunities. Visitors can explore the canyon on foot via the Riverside Walk or embark on more challenging hikes such as The Narrows, a famous slot canyon hike through the river.

Angels Landing

Angels Landing is one of Zion's most iconic hikes, known for its breathtaking views and exhilarating ascent. The trail ascends steep switchbacks and exposed cliffs before reaching a narrow ridge with sheer drop-offs on both sides. The final section, known as the "spine," involves clinging to chains bolted into the rock as you make your way to the summit. The panoramic views of Zion Canyon from the top are well worth the effort.

The Narrows

The Narrows is a unique hiking experience unlike any other in Zion National Park. This slot canyon hike follows the course of the Virgin River, with towering walls rising hundreds of feet on either side. Most of the hike is spent wading through the river, so proper footwear and a walking stick are essential. The scenery is spectacular, with narrow passages, cascading waterfalls, and hanging gardens along the way.

Emerald Pools

The Emerald Pools are a series of lush oases nestled at the base of the towering sandstone cliffs in Zion Canyon. The Lower, Middle, and Upper Emerald Pools are accessed via interconnected trails, each offering its own unique beauty. Visitors can enjoy refreshing waterfalls, hanging gardens, and stunning views of the canyon below.

Observation Point

Observation Point is another popular hiking destination in Zion National Park, offering sweeping views of Zion Canyon from above. The trail climbs steeply to an elevation of over 6,500 feet, passing through diverse landscapes including slickrock, pine forests, and narrow canyons. The reward at the top is a panoramic vista that includes Angels Landing, the Virgin River, and the towering cliffs of Zion Canyon.

Checkerboard Mesa

Checkerboard Mesa is a unique geological feature located in the eastern part of Zion National Park. Named for its distinctive pattern of horizontal and vertical cracks resembling a checkerboard, the mesa is a popular stop along the Zion-Mt. Carmel Highway. Visitors can admire the intricate patterns of erosion on the sandstone surface and enjoy scenic views of the surrounding landscape.
